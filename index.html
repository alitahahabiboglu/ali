About Me

I am a physicist with a passion of bridging physics and engineering. I have 12+ years of experience in semiconductor research, device fabrication, and spintronics. My expertise spans device physics, fab (thin-film deposition with PVD, photolithography, etching) and physical/electrical characterization methods (SEM, AFM/MFM, XRD, PPMS, high-frequency/DC testing).

Throughout my Ph.D. at the University of Arizona and collaborations with industry partners (SRC, DARPA, NSF), I contributed to developing high-performance Magnetic Tunnel Junctions (MTJs), ultra-fast switching mechanisms, and superconducting devices. I have also built and refurbished advanced lab equipment, designed automated electrical test systems, and optimized data analysis workflow.

Research Projects (11 Projects, Led 6)

11th Project: Antiferromagnetic Tunnel Junctions (AFMTJs)

Leading R&D for low-energy and high-frequency switching. (NSF | 2022- On-going)

Managing a group of 1 Graduate and 2 undergraduate students.

  • Fabricated by PVD, Photolithography, and Ion Etching. Characterized by MOKE, VSM, XRR, XRD, and Tested.
  • Became the 4th research group in the world to fabricate, characterize, and test AFMTJs.
  • Reported preliminary data exhibiting bias dependence and Mn ratios in PtMnx AFMTJs of TMR.
  • Gained expertise in SOT switching, Antiferromagnetic thin films and devices.

10th Project: Magnon-assisted Spin Transfer Torque (STT) Switching

Leading R&D on low-energy STT switching of in-plane and perpendicular MTJs with Antiferromagnetic Barrier (NiO). (DARPA (Franc) | 2022 - 2024)

Managing a group of 1 Graduate and 1 undergraduate students. Patented the technology.

  • Fabricated, Characterized, and Tested using PVD, Photolithography (150nm), Ion Etching, MOKE, VSM, XRR, XRD.
  • Optimized the Oxygen ratio in NiO using reactive RF sputtering to obtain the desired stoichiometry and PMA.
  • Achieved the highest (26%) TMR among NiO-based MTJs.
  • Acquired expertise in STT switching, Magnons, and Antiferromagnets.
