About Me

I am a physicist with a passion of bridging physics and engineering. I bring over 12 years of experience in semiconductor research, nano-device fabrication, spintronics with my knowledge in thin-film deposition and integration of emerging materials. My expertise spans device physics, fabriation (thin-film deposition with PVD, photolithography, etching) and physical/electrical characterization methods (SEM, AFM/MFM, XRD, PPMS, high-frequency/DC testing).

Throughout my Ph.D. at the University of Arizona and collaborations with industry partners (SRC, DARPA, NSF), I contributed to developing high-performance, low energy nano-scale devices by utilizing novel physical mechanisms and materials in Magnetic Tunnel Junctions (MTJs). I am interested in developing and optimizing high-entropy coatings tailored for the extreme environments encountered in fusion technology, as well as graphene nanoribbon‚Äìbased devices, specifically exploring the potential of GNR diagnostic sensors for first-wall applications. Also, I have built, and refurbished thin film fabrication and processing systems, designed and set-up automated electrical test systems, and optimized data analysis workflow.

10th Project: Magnon-assisted Spin Transfer Torque (STT) Switching

Leading R&D on low-energy STT switching of in-plane and perpendicular MTJs with Antiferromagnetic Barrier (NiO). (DARPA (Franc) | 2022 - 2024)

Managing a group of 1 Graduate and 1 undergraduate students. Patented the technology.

  • Fabricated, Characterized, and Tested using PVD, Photolithography (150nm), Ion Etching, MOKE, VSM, XRR, XRD.
  • Optimized the Oxygen ratio in NiO using reactive RF sputtering to obtain the desired stoichiometry and PMA.
  • Achieved the highest (26%) TMR among NiO-based MTJs.
  • Acquired expertise in STT switching, Magnons, and Antiferromagnets.
